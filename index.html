Indian Knowledge System — Questions & Answers

1. How was the temple architecture in the ancient period?
Indian temple architecture in the ancient period was highly advanced, symbolic, and technically sophisticated. It followed perfect proportions, geometry, and alignment with astronomical principles. Temples had Garbhagriha (sanctum), Mandapa (assembly hall), Shikhara or Vimana (tower), Pradakshina Patha, intricate sculptures, and carvings. Materials like stone, granite, and sandstone were used with precise engineering. Temples also reflected spiritual themes, cultural values, and regional artistic styles such as Nagara, Dravida, and Vesara architecture.
2. Discuss the importance and scope of Indian Knowledge System.
Indian Knowledge System (IKS) is important because it preserves holistic wisdom developed over thousands of years. It provides insights on health, environment, ethics, governance, mathematics, astronomy, arts, and social values. Its scope includes Ayurveda, Yoga, Metallurgy, Architecture, Education, Agriculture, Economic systems, and Philosophy. IKS helps understand sustainable practices, moral living, scientific thinking, and cultural heritage. It offers modern relevance in health sciences, technology, environmental conservation, and value-based education.
3. Explain the major IKS texts.
Major IKS texts include: • *Vedas* – foundational knowledge on philosophy, rituals, science, and society. • *Upanishads* – explain spiritual knowledge and concepts like Brahman and Atman. • *Ramayana & Mahabharata* – epics teaching ethics, duty, leadership, and governance. • *Puranas* – preserve historical, mythological, cosmological knowledge. • *Ayurveda texts* like Charaka Samhita and Sushruta Samhita – basis of Indian medical science. • *Arthashastra* – ancient treatise on economics, polity, statecraft. • *Yoga Sutras* – give psychological and physical discipline methods.
4. Show the classification of knowledge in Indian traditions.
Indian traditions classify knowledge into several categories: 1. *Śruti* – divine revelations including Vedas and Upanishads. 2. *Smriti* – memory-based texts like Manusmriti, Dharma Shastras. 3. *Itihasa* – historical epics such as Ramayana and Mahabharata. 4. *Purana* – encyclopedic literature with cultural and philosophical stories. 5. *Darshana* – six schools of Indian philosophy. 6. *Shastras* – practical sciences like Ayurveda, Arthashastra, Vastu Shastra etc. This classification reflects India's vast and systematic knowledge organisation.
5. What do you mean by polity and administrations?
Polity refers to the system of governance, political structures, laws, and institutions that guide society. Administration means the implementation of policies, management of resources, and functioning of government machinery. Together they ensure social order, justice, welfare, and effective governance in a state.
6. What are the sources of ancient Indian polity?
Major sources include: • Vedas and Brahmanas – concepts of kingship and duties. • Dharmashastras and Smritis – legal and ethical guidelines. • Arthashastra – detailed study of polity, economy, and military. • Epics like Ramayana and Mahabharata – ideals of good governance. • Buddhist and Jain texts – moral and administrative principles. • Inscriptions and edicts – practical governance models. These sources provide a comprehensive understanding of ancient Indian administration.
7. What are the features of Ancient Indian Polity?
Ancient Indian polity was based on Dharma, moral rule, and welfare of people. Key features include: • King as protector of Dharma but guided by laws. • Council of ministers for administration. • Panchayat system encouraging local self-governance. • Strong justice system with fair taxation. • Emphasis on social harmony, equality, and ethical conduct. This system balanced power, duty, and welfare.
8. Explain the significance of Ancient Indian Polity.
Ancient Indian polity is significant because it introduced ethical leadership, structured administration, and welfare-focused governance. It gave concepts of justice, taxation, diplomacy, and public welfare. Texts like Arthashastra provided advanced political science knowledge. Democratic institutions like sabhas and samitis show early participatory governance. These principles still influence modern political systems.
9. Discuss Ancient Indian Education System.
The ancient education system focused on holistic learning, discipline, and character-building. Gurukula system taught students values, self-control, and practical skills. Universities like Takshashila and Nalanda were global learning centers teaching medicine, mathematics, astronomy, grammar, and philosophy. Education was free, inclusive, and aimed at developing intellectual, moral, and spiritual abilities.
10. Explain the Value Based Education in Indian Tradition.
Indian tradition emphasized value-based learning including truth, non-violence, discipline, compassion, humility, respect for elders, and service to society. The purpose was to develop good character, ethical behavior, and emotional maturity. These values enabled students to become responsible citizens and morally strong individuals.
11. Role of teacher and student in society.
Teachers were respected guides who provided knowledge, wisdom, discipline, and moral values. Students were expected to maintain discipline, follow rules, show respect, and devote themselves to learning. Together they contributed to societal progress, cultural preservation, and ethical development.
12. What do you mean by Indian Knowledge System?
Indian Knowledge System refers to India’s holistic, traditional, and scientific knowledge developed over thousands of years. It includes philosophy, health systems, mathematics, astronomy, arts, culture, architecture, and environmental wisdom. IKS connects science with ethics, spirituality, and sustainable living.
13. Influence of Shilpa Shastras on Khajuraho Temples.
Shilpa Shastras provided rules regarding temple proportions, measurements, sculptures, and carvings. The Khajuraho temples followed these principles to achieve symmetry, beauty, and expressive art. The texts guided iconography, posture, and artistic detailing, resulting in aesthetically perfect structures.
14. Write architectural marvels of India.
India has many architectural marvels such as: • Ajanta and Ellora caves • Konark Sun Temple • Khajuraho Group of Monuments • Badami, Aihole, Pattadakal temples • Jagannath Temple, Puri These monuments show advanced engineering, artistic excellence, and cultural richness.
15. Explain town planning of ancient India.
Ancient Indian towns had grid-pattern roads, efficient drainage systems, public baths, wells, markets, and residential areas. Cities like Harappa and Mohenjo-daro used baked bricks, underground drains, and planned streets. Town planning reflected cleanliness, organization, sustainability, and civic responsibility.
16. How used machines in ancient India?
Ancient India used machines like water-lifting devices (shaduf), irrigation systems, stepwells, oil presses, and mechanical tools. Architects used pulley systems, ramps, and lever mechanisms to construct large temples and monuments. These technologies show advanced engineering and scientific understanding.
17. What is the Surya Siddhanta?
Surya Siddhanta is an ancient Indian treatise on astronomy. It explains planetary motions, eclipses, solstices, equinoxes, time calculation, and trigonometric concepts. It was highly accurate and influenced global astronomical traditions for centuries.
18. Explain paints & dyes in IKS.
Indian Knowledge System used natural dyes and paints derived from plants, minerals, and insects. Examples include indigo, turmeric, saffron, vermilion, malachite, and lac. These dyes were eco-friendly, long-lasting, and used in textiles, paintings, sculpture, and rituals.
19. Entrepreneurship in organic farming (IKS).
IKS promotes organic farming using cow-based fertilizers, natural pest control, and indigenous seeds. Entrepreneurship includes selling organic produce, herbal products, bio-fertilizers, and sustainable farming tools. It improves soil health and supports eco-friendly agriculture.
20. Role of IKS in Ayurveda start-ups.
IKS inspires Ayurveda-based startups focusing on herbal healthcare, organic foods, skincare, nutraceuticals, and wellness products. Companies like Patanjali, Himalaya, and MamaEarth use ancient Ayurvedic principles. It promotes natural healing and global recognition of Indian traditional medicine.
21. Chipko Movement.
Chipko Movement was a forest conservation movement started in the 1970s in Uttarakhand. Villagers, especially women, hugged trees to prevent their cutting by contractors. It highlighted environmental protection, community participation, and sustainable development.
